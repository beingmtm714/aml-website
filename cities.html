The partnership
What we need from you
  • A designated liaison to help us reach local arts and cultural channels. One point of contact who knows the community.
  • In-kind venue support for the 3-day intensive, or introductions to local venues and arts centers. We'll work with what's available — a library, a community center, a university space.
  • Promotion through city channels — newsletters, social, email lists — to reach local artists. We write the copy. You send it to your audience.
  • Conversations with city and arts stakeholders so we can tailor the Lab to your city's creative scene. Two or three calls before the program launches.
What your city gets
  • Economic development: new creative micro-businesses paying into the local tax base. Real businesses, not just participants with certificates.
  • Equity and access: institutional-quality business training for creative entrepreneurs, free for participants. The same curriculum taught at Berklee College of Music.
  • Civic branding: your city's logo on certificates, marketing materials, and a public showcase. Visible, documented, shareable.
  • Ongoing artist resources: alumni network, toolkit, and continuing access to sponsor-provided tools. The infrastructure stays after the program ends.
  • Full delivery by our team: curriculum, facilitation, coaching, and follow-up. You open the door. We run the program.

After the Lab,
the infrastructure
stays.

The program ends. Your city leaves with trained entrepreneurs, an alumni network, a toolkit, and a cohort of people who know how to teach what they learned to the next group.

We're building a seed for a local creative innovation center and we jumpstart it for you. Whether your city continues to grow it is up to you — but you'll have the people, the knowledge, and the model to do it. We stay available to help cities build on what the Lab starts.
