Why now

What we teach

Your craft is yours. The business
around it is what we teach.

Most artisans can discuss their craft at length, but few have learned pricing or contracts. The program begins there: pricing, packaging, contracts, invoicing, distribution, audience-building, and revenue left unpriced.

Then build what you used to rent or go without:

  • A glassblower's commission-and-inventory tracker
  • A leatherworker's custom-order intake system
  • A brewer's taproom loyalty tool

Understanding your business is the foundation for building the tools that run it. A local operator in your city helped shape the curriculum, so it reflects your market rather than a generic template.

AI tools that generate creative work are not part of the syllabus; tools that provide infrastructure are.

Half of a
guild was
always
people.

Software supports the workshop half. It cannot replace the people.

Guilds lasted because members trained and vouched for each other, and spoke with one voice when it mattered. Every cohort builds that network locally: artisans, a responsive SBA loan officer, a city planner, a bank willing to open accounts easily.

A workshop ends when the facilitator leaves. This does not. A city that funds Open Guild invests in infrastructure artisans rely on for years.

phases.

03

  • Structured sprints with weekly office hours

04
